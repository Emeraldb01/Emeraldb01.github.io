Tracing the Emotional Impact of Dark Humor

A Longitudinal Analysis of Reddit Users Sentiment Before and After Commenting on Dark Memes


Year: 2025 | Team Project | Role: Product Manager & Data Engineer


This ongoing research explores how exposure to dark humor memes relates to changes in user sentiment, engagement, and online well-being. By analyzing large-scale Reddit discussions, our goal is to understand whether engaging with darker meme content correlates with emotional adaptation, stress relief, or increased negativity over time.

As Product Manager and Data Engineer, I designed the project’s technical pipeline and led research coordination. I built automated scripts to scrape, clean, and categorize Reddit data using PRAW and Pandas, and integrated sentiment and emotion scoring models including VADER and LIWC. I also managed research milestones, authored the project charter and midterm progress report, and established reproducible workflows for team analysis.

Our research bridges computational social science and mental health — investigating how humor, exposure to dark content, and emotional expression interact in online spaces. The long-term goal is to inform healthier content moderation and help platforms distinguish between dark humor as a coping mechanism versus harmful negativity.


Key Deliverables:

    - Designed and implemented end-to-end Reddit data collection and classification pipeline
    - Defined success metrics and project documentation for academic reproducibility
    - Conducted sentiment, toxicity, and emotional category analysis using LIWC and VADER
    - Coordinated midterm milestone reporting and visualized early statistical findings


Current Progress: At the midterm stage, the dataset includes over 10,000 posts from dark and non-dark meme subreddits. Preliminary analysis suggests measurable differences in emotional tone and linguistic expression between groups, forming the foundation for final hypothesis testing.
