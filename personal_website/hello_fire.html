HELlo_fire: Sustainable Firebreak Simulation

Modeling Wildfire Spread and Tractor Path Planning for Safer, Smarter Farming


Year: 2025 | Team Project | Role: Product Manager & Full Stack Developer


HELlo_fire is an interactive research simulation that models wildfire behavior on farmland and computes optimal tractor firebreak paths to protect crops and reduce environmental damage. The project explores how digital agriculture and autonomous systems can contribute to farm safety, sustainability, and data-driven decision making.

As Product Manager and Full Stack Developer, I led both strategic planning and technical implementation. I coordinated our milestone roadmap, authored the project charter and success criteria, and managed documentation to align research goals with real-world applications. On the development side, I built the simulation engine that integrates terrain, slope, wind, and moisture data with fire propagation models, enabling tractors to adaptively compute safer paths in dynamic fire conditions.

Our long-term goal is to connect this research to real tractor sensor data and control systems, helping farmers make informed decisions that minimize loss while improving worker safety.
